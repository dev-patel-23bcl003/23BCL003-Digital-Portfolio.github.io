Interdisciplinary Projects and Research

Major project work demonstrating problem-solving across multiple fields.

1. Sustainable Smart Campus Drainage and Rainwater Harvesting Plan

Project Overview: The objective was to prepare a sustainable drainage concept for a campus area by combining Civil Engineering, Environmental Engineering, hydrology, and basic data analysis.

Methodology

Site observation, rainfall data study, catchment area calculation, runoff estimation, and layout planning.

Outcomes & Innovation

Proposed rainwater harvesting pits, stormwater flow direction, and eco-friendly water management strategy.

Visual / Prototype

Concept layout, flow arrows, water collection sketch, and calculation table.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: managing runoff during high rainfall. Solution: combining surface drains with recharge pits.

Visual Artefact: Drainage layout + rainfall-runoff calculation sheet

2. Simulation-Based Optimization of Taxiway Design

Project Overview: This project connected Transportation Engineering, airport planning, simulation thinking, and operational efficiency to understand how taxiway layout affects aircraft movement and delays.

Methodology

Studied airport taxiway elements, compared layout alternatives, identified movement conflicts, and used simple simulation logic.

Outcomes & Innovation

Suggested an optimized taxiway arrangement to reduce waiting time, improve aircraft circulation, and support safer movement.

Visual / Prototype

Taxiway sketches, comparison table, flow diagram, and presentation slides.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: balancing safety and efficiency. Solution: clear conflict points and movement paths were identified.

Visual Artefact: Taxiway movement diagram + layout comparison

3. Solar EV Charging Station Proposal for Sustainable Infrastructure

Methodology

Prepared concept layout, basic civil work requirements, cost components, energy requirement, and implementation plan.

Outcomes & Innovation

Visual / Prototype

Global Awareness and Ethical Considerations

How global issues and ethical thinking influence engineering work.

Global Perspective

Civil Engineering is directly connected with climate change, urbanization, resource use, safety, and public welfare. Projects such as drainage planning, EV infrastructure, and airport design require awareness of environmental impact and long-term sustainability.

Case Scenario: A low-cost design may reduce initial expense, but if it ignores drainage, safety, or durability, it can create long-term risk for users and the environment.

Ethical Reflection

As an engineer, ethical responsibility includes accuracy in calculations, honest estimation, safe design, proper material selection, and respect for society and the environment. A good engineer must think beyond completion of work and consider the impact of decisions on people.

Personal View: I believe engineering success should be measured by safety, usefulness, sustainability, and public benefit.
